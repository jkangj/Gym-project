Bench Press Tutorial

Warning: Always use a spotter when bench pressing and ensure you are lifting a manageable weight to avoid injury.

Step 1: Starting Position

Lie flat on a bench with your feet planted firmly on the floor. Grip the barbell slightly wider than shoulder-width, with your arms fully extended.

Arm Angle: Begin with an arm angle of approximately 160 degrees between your shoulder, elbow, and wrist.

Step 2: Lower the Barbell

Slowly lower the barbell to your chest while keeping your elbows at a 45-degree angle to your torso.

Target Arm Angle: Lower the barbell until the angle between your shoulder, elbow, and wrist is approximately 80 degrees.

Step 3: Press the Barbell Up

Push the barbell back up to the starting position in a controlled manner. Focus on engaging your chest and triceps.

Return to Starting Arm Angle: Return to an arm angle of approximately 160 degrees at the top of the press.

Step 4: Complete the Repetition

Ensure each repetition takes at least 1 second to complete. Avoid locking your elbows at the top to maintain tension on your muscles.
